Product Case Study

Heading Home: Reducing Driver Deadhead on Rapido

Concept response to a community product pitch, reworked using CIRCLES to stress-test the idea before proposing a build.

Problem

Drivers often end their shift by driving home empty with no additional fare, no utilization, pure cost. This pushes drivers to log off earlier than they otherwise would, shrinking supply right when evening demand is still active.

Root Cause

It's not that drivers don't want more rides near the end of shift instead it's that today's matching engine is direction-blind. A driver heading home may get matched to a ride that pulls them further away, so going fully offline feels safer than risking a bad match.

Users & Conflicting Needs

Driver
Earn on the way home, minimal detour
Rider
Fast match, doesn't care about driver's destination
Rapido
Higher utilization, lower idle-km, without hurting rider wait time

Solution Options Considered

ApproachTrade-off
Hard corridor filter (only show rides toward home)Simple, but starves riders in low-density zones
Soft bias in matching scoreBalances both sides, chosen for version1 (v1)
"One more ride" toggle, no address neededCheapest to build, doesn't target deadhead-km directly

Recommendation: soft bias, capped so rider ETA never degrades beyond a small threshold additionally with home location fuzzed to an area, not an exact pin, and abuse checks against gaming the destination input.

Guardrails Before Rollout

  • Cap rider wait-time increase (e.g. no more than a small % delay)
  • Fuzz driver's home to a zone, not an exact address
  • Detect drivers repeatedly changing "home" to game better rides
  • Always allow full offline as fallback if no match appears in time
